London's 1854 Cholera epidemic

About

The Street Map of Cholera Deaths visualization is a modern representation of Dr. John Snow's map of the 1854 London cholera outbreak. The original map was used to establish a link between contaminated water and cholera. He used bars to represent deaths that occurred at specified households, along with water pumps represented by circles. The new visualization I provide has small circles that represent each death and large circles that represent the water pumps. I also provide three graphs to filter the map between male and female deaths, specific dates, and deaths by age ranges.

Instructions for Vizualization

Click to select single or combinations of variables on charts. Once variables are selected on chart, variables will appear black. Zoom in/out or scroll around on the map by using mouse. Press reset to show all variables on map. Hover over circle elements that represent deaths to view details.
